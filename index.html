About the Project

CRIMA is a research project focused on building a next-generation decision-support system for climate and environmental risk management. It integrates diverse sources of information such as satellite Earth Observation (EO) data, climate models, scientific literature, and expert knowledge into a single Virtual Knowledge Graph (VKG).

The VKG enables users to explore and interpret complex environmental risks using intuitive tools. These include AI-powered interfaces that support natural language and structured queries, as well as conceptual models like impact chains that help visualize how different risk components are connected.

CRIMA focuses on accessibility for non-technical users, such as local planners and decision-makers. It allows them to ask high-level questions like how a specific region may be affected by increasing drought conditions, and receive meaningful, data-driven answers.

The system is being tested in two real-world application scenarios in the Alpine region. One focuses on hydrometeorological hazards such as floods and landslides. The other focuses on long-term risks related to water scarcity.
