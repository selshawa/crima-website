About the Project

CRIMA focuses on the development of a decision-support system to enhance climate and environmental risk management. The system semantically integrates heterogeneous sources of information using a Virtual Knowledge Graph (VKG) and is powered by AI-supported querying of Big Earth Data.

It aims to help non-technical users query and interpret complex environmental data through natural language and conceptual models like impact chains. The system will be evaluated in two application scenarios in the Alpine region: one on hydrometeorological events, and one on water scarcity.

Project Objectives

  • Develop a VKG that integrates quantitative Earth Observation (EO) data and scientific literature using semantic web technologies.
  • Enable AI-assisted querying and analysis of Big Earth Data.
  • Apply innovative conceptual models (e.g., impact chains) to describe climate risk components and their interrelations.
  • Use openEO APIs and extensions to support data access and processing.
  • Develop user-friendly interfaces including SPARQL and LLM/NLP querying.
  • Demonstrate the system in real-world application scenarios.

Research Questions

The research is based on three pillars: VKG data modeling, impact chain conceptualization, and integration with openEO.

  1. How can impact chains be formally modeled within VKGs?
  2. How can openEO processes be integrated into VKG queries, and which parts should be delegated?
  3. How can dynamic EO data be semantically linked to VKG instances of impact chain concepts?

Challenges and Opportunities

  • Integrating multiple knowledge domains: environmental data, vulnerability, and adaptation measures.
  • Making a rich body of scientific knowledge accessible and usable by stakeholders.
  • Supporting non-technical users through intuitive interfaces and semantic tools.

Synergies

CRIMA contributes to climate change adaptation planning in South Tyrol and the broader Alpine region. It supports key SDG priorities: Sustainable Cities and Communities (SDG 11), Climate Action (SDG 13), and Life on Land (SDG 15).

Timeline and Milestones

The official project kick-off took place in February 2025. The project spans 36 months and includes the following milestones:

  • Milestone 1 (Month 4): Requirements analysis
  • Milestone 2 (Month 20): Proof of concept – Basic CRIMA system with impact chain modeling and limited query capabilities
  • Milestone 3 (Month 32): Final system – Complete VKG integration, full user interface, ready for pilot scenarios
  • Milestone 4 (Month 36): Evaluation of system through stakeholder testing and demonstration

Partners

The CRIMA project is led by Eurac Research, coordinating project management, stakeholder engagement, development of openEO, and the application scenarios. The Free University of Bozen-Bolzano leads the development of semantic models and the integration of openEO with the Virtual Knowledge Graph. Ontopic is responsible for implementing natural language and LLM-based interfaces for querying the system.

Funding

CRIMA is funded by the European Regional Development Fund (FESR).
